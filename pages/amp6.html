my 41hz.com amp6 project

I'm building a little stereo amp based on the 41hz.com amp6 kit. I'm doing this because typical stereo receivers waste a lot of power due to being class A/B amplifiers, they're somewhat-annoyingly large, and just because I like designing/building stuff. I'm not sure if I'll be able to notice a difference in sound since I don't consider myself to have great hearing -- not to mention the fact that I'm generally listening to music from a computer with cheap on-board sound -- but the 41hz amps are considered to have very high fidelity, as are other amps based on Tripath chips in general. I'm spending more than the minimum for decent quality parts, although it is still very cheap by "audiophile" standards. The gold rca jacks and binding posts, noble potentiometer, and tocos 4pdt switches are all of higher quality than I could have gotten away with if I wanted to be extremely cheap. But input signals in particular are very susceptible to the quality of the components they run through, so everything the signal travels through before arriving at the amp board has either silver or gold contacts. Another issue is "vampire power" or the trickle of power consumption that many devices exhibit when apparently turned off. This amp will be designed so that the power switch will cut off the a/c connection and no transformers or anything else will be consuming any energy when it is switched off.

controls/inputs/outputs

back panel:
  • a/c power input
  • two pairs of speaker binding posts
  • four pairs of rca inputs
front panel:
  • power switch
  • source 1/2/none toggle switch
  • source 3/4/none toggle switch
  • volume knob
